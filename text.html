I like to write. ♾️ hello everyone. first I thank you all for your kindness. these past few years were spent in growth, always. to old faces, I am grateful for the history; with newer ones, I want to share this side of me with you all, finally. it’s a longrunning joke among friends that I’ve taken a comically long hiatus from something I claim to love so much. in hindsight yes it was odd. maybe it was partially cowardice; my previous forays into music were met with mixed reactions, and I became wary of opinions. but I never stepped away from creating. and it was in the years I spent away from releasing new art that I began growing into the artist I always dreamed of being. I learned that life is the greatest source material. I always struggled to write songs back in high. now I have so much to say… please accept this song, from me, to you. it was written with care, and also hastily, about a woman and a dream, both equally important yet impossibly discordant. with passion always riding shotgun and my nagging perfectionism temporarily relegated to the backseat. I met someone a summer ago, and I’ve had the dream for a much longer time than that. but please don’t think about it too hard. many of my favorite songs, by the greats, hardly remember their authorial intent. they exist solely as the soundtrack to millions of concurrent films, billions of hearts pounding, trillions of neurons firing away.. wallowed in creative debts, I could never be shy about citing my influences. nostalgia ultra, the marvelous display of interpolation that it was, inspired me to also look to personal teachers. this track in particular owes its existence to strawberry swing by Coldplay, and Frank’s version of course. I’ve been mesmerized for years by that song’s driving force, forlorn qualities, lyrics. the full project (to come soon) is a love letter to decades of various musical legacies. artists have a power and duty to regenerate, so by revisiting these people’s works you honor me more deeply than I could ever ask for. ahead of the coming weeks, years, eternity: thank you for your ears and even the slightest bit of interest. I am nothing without others. In time, I hope my music will prove similarly codependent on other intelligent, breathing, loving human beings. - j infinite.

03.05.2025

I fell in love with vaporwave in senior year of high school. Years later, it remains a personal obsession. I started where I think most people begin -- floral shoppe by macintosh plus, and other by-chance encounters on youtube. It also helped that one of my friends became a defining pillar of the sound. I know and recognize the original intent behind the phenomenon, but it was honestly never satirical for me. I really loved the music.

What makes it work is a genuine, inimitable yearning for the nonexistent. The genre samples music from the last century, so the nostalgia is already there. But to me it's different from hip-hop or house or other genres that sample because it's not trying to make something new and exciting from the past. Instead it submits to yesterday, it invites me to want something I never had: anemoia. While I hate when other genres "lazy-sample", I actually prefer it when vaporwave leaves the track mostly alone. It pays homage to the original song but forces it to live in the present, like a man out of time. Vaporwave is a time machine that bends reality - I'm listening to Diana Ross, city pop, and water level OSTs from '90s games, warped beyond recognition or sometimes just slowed a bit, playing in the fuzzy texture of a dream. Three songs into the playlist, I can almost believe that I grew up pre-iPhone and social media hegemony.

It's a gateway drug too, the genre is by nature a transmitter more than anything so it begs you to explore the legends of yesterday, it screams at you to check out what the past sounds like un-slowed and un-manipulated.

But what vaporwave will be remembered for by future vaporologists is the feeling. And here I urge you to discard the original satirical intentions, because they are long forgotten and abandoned. those of us who have stayed behind don't listen to floral shoppe to applaud the caricaturization of capitalism. We flock to the reverb-gated snares and dreamy synths of the past to feel something. Vaporwave is an escape. It was an escape for me then, and it still is now.
